A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India


Early Life of APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr APJ Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He lived in a big family with modest means. He was the youngest of four brothers and a sister. He was a brilliant and hardworking student with an interest in mathematics. He recounted the story of how his interest in flight and aerodynamics was sparked by one of his teachers in school. He also worked part-time since early childhood, selling newspapers, to supplement his family’s meagre income. After completing his school education, Kalam studied Physics and aerospace engineering in college.


Career
APJ Abdul Kalam dreamed of becoming a fighter pilot in the Indian Air Force. He narrowly missed achieving his dream as a youngster but went on to serve the nation through his pioneering work in missile technology. Dr Kalam began his career as a scientist at Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He also worked with the renowned space scientist, Dr Vikram Sarabhai. After this, he went on to work with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He worked on developing a Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV) and Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle that was a remarkable milestone in the Indian space programme. He also worked on Agni and Prithvi missile that proved India’s mettle as a nuclear state. Not only did he leave his mark in the field of space and military but also in the field of medicine where he developed a low-cost coronary stent along with a cardiologist and a tablet computer for fortifying healthcare in rural areas.


President of India
Kalam came to be the 11th President of India. He was sworn in on 25 July 2002. He was a people’s president. He worked tirelessly to bring India at par with other nuclear superpowers and also strengthened diplomatic ties with countries like the US and Russia. He was a firm believer of treating every individual equally regardless of caste, religion or gender. Despite his popularity among masses, he decided to step away from politics after his first term and did not contest elections for a second term.


Books
APJ Abdul Kalam authored many books in his lifetime and each is a veritable treasure. Some of his most notable works include Ignited Minds, Wings of Fire – an autobiography, Indomitable Spirit, India 2020, Target 3 Billion. Each of his books is a must-read for anyone who wishes to live and serve the nation like the humble person Kalam was.
